2. CORE MECHANICS (The Missing Rules)
Add these definitions to the "Core Mechanics" section or create an "Appendix: Game Rules" section.
Hand & Resource Limits
Maximum Hand Size: The maximum hand size is 10 cards. If a player holds more than 10 cards at the end of their turn, they must discard down to 10.
Maximum Spektra: The Spektra Pile is capped at 10 cards. Players cannot add more Avatars to the pile once this limit is reached.
Deck Out (Fatigue): If a player attempts to draw a card from an empty deck, they take Fatigue Damage.
Effect: The player's Active Avatar takes 1 damage (increasing by +1 for each subsequent draw attempt).
Deck Building Restrictions
Mythic Limit: While standard cards allow up to 4 copies, Mythic Rarity cards are limited to 1 copy per deck to preserve game balance.
Life Card Origin
Setup: At the start of the match, after decks are shuffled, players draw the top 4 cards of their deck and place them face-down as Life Cards. These are removed from the drawable deck count.
Equipment & Item Logic
Item Usage: There is no limit to the number of Item cards played per turn, provided the player has the Spektra to pay for them. (Remove the "Limit 1" rule at ).
Equipment Capacity: An Avatar can hold a maximum of 2 Equipment Cards at any time.
Overwriting: If an Avatar is at full capacity, the player may choose to destroy an existing attached Equipment card to make room for a new one.
3. GAMEPLAY LOGIC (Developer Specifications)
These rules clarify the "gray areas" in the Combat and Turn Structure sections.
Combat State & Switching
Switching Exhaustion: When an Avatar is moved from Reserve to the Active slot (via the "Switching" action ), it enters the field Exhausted.
Rule: It cannot attack this turn unless it possesses a specific skill (e.g., "Ambush" or "Haste").
Exception: This does not apply to Avatars promoted automatically after a death; only manual switching triggers Exhaustion.
Turn Phasing & Death
Main Phase 2 Resources: Spektra does not refresh at the start of Main Phase 2. Players must save unspent Spektra from Main Phase 1 if they wish to use cards after the Battle Phase.
Immediate Promotion: If an Active Avatar is defeated during the Damage Phase, the "Recheck" happens instantaneously. The player must promote a Reserve Avatar immediately before play proceeds to Main Phase 2. There is never an "Empty Board" state during a Main Phase.
Card Zones & Interactions
The Spektra Pile: Cards placed in the Spektra Pile are considered "Exiled Energy."
They lose their Avatar stats/types.
They cannot be targeted, destroyed, or returned to the hand/deck by any card effect.
Reserve Overflow: If a player's Reserve slots are full (Max 2), any effect that attempts to summon or spawn a unit into the Reserve fails (the spell fizzles or the card returns to hand).
Graveyard Logic: When an Avatar is defeated or an Equipment card is destroyed, it moves to the Graveyard.
Status & Stats
Negative Stat Floor: An Avatar's Attack or Health cannot drop below 0. If a debuff would reduce stats below 0, they remain at 0. (Attacks with 0 Attack deal 0 damage).
Status Timing: Damage-over-time effects (Burn, Bleed) trigger during the End Phase.
Freeze Definition: A "Frozen" Avatar cannot Attack, use Active Skills, or Evolve. It can still be swapped to the Reserve manually.
Evolution Math: When an Avatar evolves, existing damage counters persist.
Calculation: New Current HP = Old Current HP + (New Max HP - Old Max HP).
Special Card Types
Field Cards: Field Cards are Global Unique. Only one Field Card can be active at a time. If a player plays a new Field Card, the existing Field Card (regardless of owner) is destroyed. But the cost of the Field Card will +1 from its card cost
